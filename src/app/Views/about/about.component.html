FAQ

Do I have to register to use this app?

No! You can use the app without registering! However, if you don't register and you want to use Spotify services or cloud services, you'll have to login everytime.

If you register, however, you won't need to login to your platforms anymore, and your library will be loaded automatically when you log in.

Is it free?

Yes! This is still in an early state, so it's free for anyone to use.

Dropbox/OneDrive API

OLMA will let you connect to OneDrive or Dropbox to have read/write access to your Documents/Traktor 3.X.X folder, where your Traktor collection file is (collection.nml).

The idea is that, providing that you have your local music files synchronized to a cloud service, you could open your collection.nml from anywhere.

You can connect to those services either as a guest (you’ll have to login every time to retrieve your collection file), or as an app registered user. If it's the latter, as soon as you login to my app, you’ll be automatically connected to your cloud storage service.

Spotify API

The app can also connect to Spotify. Why would you connect it to Spotify? Because my app will automatically try to pair each track of your collection to its correspondent track in Spotify.

If successfully paired, it will automatically fetch advanced metadata generated by Spotify (through Machine Learning algorithms?) such as:

  • Popularity
  • Danceability
  • Energy
  • Acousticness
  • Instrumentalness

And why do you want all this parameters?

Well, the purpose of my app is to allow you to create even more powerful smartlists than Traktor’s own, allowing you to:

  • Create combined and/or Smartlists
  • Create Smartlists with more than 8 rules.
  • Create Smartlists with custom metadata fields (such as the Danceability field).

How does it work?

It allows you to upload your local library from your local files or from a connected cloud storage service. It then parses your library (.nml, the file extension of your Traktor collection, is essentially an XML file) and it shows it online. It also shows your playlists and your smartlists with its rules.

Once uploaded and parsed, you can then edit your smartlists (adding new rules to it) or create new ones.

But what if your smartlists has more than 8 rules, or has conditions that don’t exist in Traktor Pro? Don't worry! When it exports your files, if the Smartlist doesn't meat Traktor's parameters, it will export it as a regular playlist.

The idea is that, in the future, if you are registered, the next time you upload your Library again, my app recognized those Smartlists (which will be regular playlists by then) by their UUID, and recreate them as smartlists with the conditions you originally set!

Finally, once you are done, it allows you to download it again to your computer, or to upload it directly to your Cloud service.

NOTE: Parsing/fetching/downloading processes may take some time, it will depend on the size of your Library.
